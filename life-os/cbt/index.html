Growth Zone

CBT

Growth hacks from Cognitive Behavioral Therapy to challenge negative thoughts and boost positive actions.

7 practical entries

  • Create a List of Activities That Give You a Sense of Accomplishment (eCreate a list of activities that give you a sense of accomplishment (e.g., cooking a meal, organizing a shelf). Try one today.
  • Notice When You Feel Like Avoiding SomethingNotice when you feel like avoiding something. Do the opposite action (e.g., if you feel like withdrawing, send a message to a friend).
  • Pick a Hobby or Interest You’ve Neglected (ePick a hobby or interest you’ve neglected (e.g., playing an instrument, gardening). Spend 10–15 minutes on it today.
  • Pick One Activity You Enjoy or Used to Enjoy (ePick one activity you enjoy or used to enjoy (e.g., walking, painting, playing a game). Schedule it for today, even if you don’t feel like it.
  • Reach Out to Someone (eReach out to someone (e.g., call a friend, join a group activity). Plan a social connection, even if it’s brief.
  • Set One Small, Achievable Goal for the Day (eSet one small, achievable goal for the day (e.g., ‘Clean one drawer’ or ‘Call a friend’). Focus on completing it.
  • Track How Your Actions Influence Your MoodTrack how your actions influence your mood. Write down what you did and how you felt after (e.g., ‘Went for a walk—felt calmer’).
