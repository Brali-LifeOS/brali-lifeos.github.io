Growth Zone

Cognitive Analytic

Growth tips from Cognitive Analytic Therapy to break unhelpful behavior cycles.

7 practical entries

  • After a Difficult Situation, Spend a Few Minutes Reflecting on What Happened, Why YouAfter a difficult situation, spend a few minutes reflecting on what happened, why you reacted as you did, and how you might handle it differently next time.
  • Draw a Map of Your Thought and Behavior Patterns, Especially Those Related to ProblemDraw a map of your thought and behavior patterns, especially those related to problem situations. Visualize how one action or thought leads to the next.
  • Notice Times When a Behavior Has a Positive Outcome, Like Getting Praise or FeelingNotice times when a behavior has a positive outcome, like getting praise or feeling accomplished. Recognize these moments to reinforce positive patterns.
  • Notice When You Feel Triggered—times When Emotions Run High or You Feel ReactiveNotice when you feel triggered—times when emotions run high or you feel reactive. Reflect on what happened and why it affected you.
  • Spend Time Reflecting on Recurring Issues in Your Life—especially in RelationshipsSpend time reflecting on recurring issues in your life—especially in relationships. Notice patterns that may be contributing to these problems.
  • When Self-Criticism Arises, Practice Reframing It with CompassionWhen self-criticism arises, practice reframing it with compassion. Instead of ‘I failed,’ try ‘I’m learning, and it’s okay to make mistakes.’
  • When You Notice a Familiar Negative Pattern, Think of One Small, Alternative Action YouWhen you notice a familiar negative pattern, think of one small, alternative action you could take instead. Experiment and notice the effects.
