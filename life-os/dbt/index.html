DBT

Learn Dialectical Behavior Therapy-inspired hacks to balance emotions and handle stress.

7 practical entries

  • Spend a Few Minutes Each Day Focusing on the PresentSpend a few minutes each day focusing on the present. Notice your breathing, your surroundings, and any thoughts that come up, without judgment.
  • Use the Dear MAN Acronym to Express Needs Assertively: Describe, Express, Assert, Reinforce, (stay)Use the DEAR MAN acronym to express needs assertively: Describe, Express, Assert, Reinforce, (stay) Mindful, Appear confident, Negotiate.
  • When a Strong Emotion Hits, Name ItWhen a strong emotion hits, name it. Say, ‘I feel angry’ or ‘I feel sad.’ Labeling emotions gives you clarity and helps reduce their intensity.
  • When Facing Something Difficult, Try Accepting It Fully Instead of ResistingWhen facing something difficult, try accepting it fully instead of resisting. Tell yourself, ‘It is what it is,’ and focus on moving forward.
  • When Stressed, Try Soothing Yourself Through Your Senses—listen to Music, Smell Something Calming, HoldWhen stressed, try soothing yourself through your senses—listen to music, smell something calming, hold something comforting, etc.
  • When You Feel a Negative Emotion, Try Doing the Opposite ActionWhen you feel a negative emotion, try doing the opposite action. If you feel sad and want to withdraw, go outside or call a friend.
  • When You’re in a Tough Moment, Use a Distress Tolerance Skill Like Deep BreathingWhen you’re in a tough moment, use a distress tolerance skill like deep breathing or holding something cold to calm down. Focus on getting through the moment without escalating.
