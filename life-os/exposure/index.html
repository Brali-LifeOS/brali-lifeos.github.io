Exposure

Face fears step-by-step with hacks inspired by Exposure Therapy to build resilience.

7 practical entries

  • Before and After Each Exposure Session, Rate Your Anxiety on a Scale of 1Before and after each exposure session, rate your anxiety on a scale of 1 to 10. Tracking helps you see progress over time as anxiety decreases.
  • For Intense Fears, Try Full Exposure (flooding) with the Help of a Supportive PersonFor intense fears, try full exposure (flooding) with the help of a supportive person. Confront the fear fully and allow the anxiety to peak and fade naturally.
  • List Your Fears from Least to Most IntenseList your fears from least to most intense. Start with the least scary and work your way up, gradually increasing your exposure as you feel more comfortable.
  • Notice When You Avoid Something Due to FearNotice when you avoid something due to fear. Commit to facing it instead, even if it’s just for a short time.
  • Start by Identifying Something You FearStart by identifying something you fear. Then break it down into smaller, manageable steps, and face each step at your own pace.
  • When Facing a Fear, Remind Yourself That You’re SafeWhen facing a fear, remind yourself that you’re safe. Repeat phrases like, ‘I am safe,’ ‘This is just a feeling,’ or ‘I can handle this.’
  • When It’s Too Hard to Face a Fear in Real Life, Imagine the SituationWhen it’s too hard to face a fear in real life, imagine the situation vividly. Focus on your feelings and let yourself experience the fear in a safe environment.
